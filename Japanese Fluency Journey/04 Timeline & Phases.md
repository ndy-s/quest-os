# Timeline & Phases — Japanese Fluency

---

## Phase 1: Kana & Foundations
**Duration:** Months 1–3 (June–August 2026)
**Motto:** Build the floor. Everything else sits on this.

### Tasks
- Weeks 1–2: Learn all hiragana and katakana to reading fluency (no romaji after week 2)
- Week 3 onward: Start Core 2000 Anki deck, 5 new cards/day
- Month 2: Begin Genki I, work through chapters 1–6 (verb conjugation, particles, basic sentences)
- Month 3: Complete Genki I chapters 7–12, start Bunpro N5 grammar points
- Throughout: 15 min of Comprehensible Japanese (absolute beginner playlist) daily from Month 2

### Readiness Gate
Before moving to Phase 2, confirm:
- [ ] Can read hiragana and katakana without hesitation (test: read a random page of Japanese text)
- [ ] 300+ words in tracker with at least one completed review cycle
- [ ] Genki I finished or equivalent N5 grammar coverage in Bunpro
- [ ] Completed at least 20 Comprehensible Japanese beginner videos

---

## Phase 2: Grammar & Vocabulary
**Duration:** Months 4–8 (September 2026–January 2027)
**Motto:** Volume and consistency. This is the grind phase.

### Tasks
- Continue Core 2000 deck, transition to Core 6000 around month 5
- Work through Genki II (chapters 13–23): te-form, conditionals, passive/causative, keigo basics
- Bunpro: complete all N4 grammar points (there are ~90)
- Listening: move to Comprehensible Japanese intermediate playlist, 20 min/day
- Month 7: Start simple reading with Satori Reader (beginner stories)
- Month 8: First iTalki lesson — just a self-introduction session to diagnose speaking gaps

### Readiness Gate
Before moving to Phase 3, confirm:
- [ ] 1000+ words in tracker with clean review history
- [ ] Genki II completed (or all N4 grammar points green in Bunpro)
- [ ] Can understand ~60% of a Comprehensible Japanese intermediate video without subtitles
- [ ] At least one successful iTalki or HelloTalk conversation

---

## Phase 3: Immersion & Speaking
**Duration:** Months 9–12 (February–May 2027)
**Motto:** Use it. Actively, out loud, with real content.

### Tasks
- Reduce new Anki cards to 3/day — focus shifts to retention and usage
- Bunpro: work through N3 grammar points (~130 points)
- Satori Reader: 20 min/day reading with dictionary
- Listening: anime or J-drama with Japanese subtitles (not English)
- Speaking: 1 iTalki lesson per week, or 3 HelloTalk exchanges per week
- Month 11: Take an official JLPT N4 practice test as a baseline
- Month 12: Take JLPT N3 (July exam registration opens in March)

### Readiness Gate / Success Criteria
- [ ] 1500+ words tracked and retained
- [ ] All N3 Bunpro grammar points at least introduced
- [ ] Can hold a 10-minute conversation on a familiar topic
- [ ] JLPT N3 practice test score above passing threshold (60/180)
- [ ] Registered for official JLPT N3 exam

---

## What Success Looks Like

By June 2027 you can: introduce yourself and have a basic conversation in Japanese, read signs, menus, and simple articles, watch anime with Japanese subtitles and follow most of the dialogue, and sit the JLPT N3 exam with a realistic chance of passing. Whether or not you pass N3 on the first attempt, you'll have built a foundation that makes continued improvement significantly easier.

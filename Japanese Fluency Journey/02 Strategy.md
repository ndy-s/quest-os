# Strategy — Japanese Fluency

## Core Principle

Japanese has four distinct skill areas: reading (kana + kanji), vocabulary, grammar, and listening/speaking. Most people fail because they try to learn all four at once without prioritizing. This plan front-loads the foundations — kana and core vocab — so every later skill builds on solid ground.

---

## Study Areas

| Area | Priority | What to Focus On | Best Resources |
|---|---|---|---|
| Kana (hiragana + katakana) | **High** (Month 1 only) | All 46+46 characters, reading fluency | Tofugu hiragana/katakana guides, Anki kana deck |
| Vocabulary | **High** (all phases) | Core words by frequency, then JLPT N5 > N4 > N3 | Anki (Core 2000/6000 deck), Wanikani (kanji+vocab) |
| Grammar | **High** (Months 2–10) | N5 > N4 > N3 grammar points in order | Genki I & II, Bunpro (SRS for grammar) |
| Listening | **Medium** (Months 3–12) | Comprehensible input at your level | Comprehensible Japanese (YouTube), podcasts, anime with JP subs |
| Speaking | **Medium** (Months 6–12) | Basic conversation, self-introduction, topic sentences | iTalki tutors, HelloTalk, Claude conversation practice |
| Kanji | **Medium** (woven in) | Learn kanji alongside vocab, not separately | Wanikani, RTK as supplement if needed |
| Reading | **Low** (Months 8–12) | Graded readers, then native text with tools | Satori Reader, NHK Web Easy |

## Weekly Schedule (7 hours/week)

| Day | Activity | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | Anki vocab review + 5 new cards | 30 min |
| Tue | Grammar study (Genki/Bunpro) + Anki | 45 min |
| Wed | Listening (Comprehensible Japanese video) + Anki | 45 min |
| Thu | Anki review + grammar review | 30 min |
| Fri | Grammar + new vocab cards | 45 min |
| Sat | Longer session: reading or speaking practice + Anki | 90 min |
| Sun | Light review only, no new items | 15 min |

**Total: ~6.5 hours.** The remaining 30 min is buffer for overdue Anki reviews — they will pile up.

---

## Key Resources

- **Anki** (free desktop, $25 iOS one-time): core SRS engine, use premade decks to start
- **Genki I & II** (~$45 each): the standard structured grammar textbook series
- **Bunpro** ($3/month): SRS specifically for grammar points, JLPT-aligned
- **Wanikani** ($9/month or lifetime deal): kanji and vocabulary with built-in SRS
- **Comprehensible Japanese** (free YouTube): graded listening, best free immersion resource
- **Satori Reader** ($7/month, Phase 3): graded reading with built-in dictionary
- **iTalki** (pay per lesson, ~$10–20/hr): human tutors for speaking practice

---

## What to Ignore (for now)

Don't touch JLPT practice tests until Month 9. Don't try to learn kanji via stroke order — learn them through vocab. Don't use Duolingo as your primary tool; it's fine as a warmup but not sufficient for N3.

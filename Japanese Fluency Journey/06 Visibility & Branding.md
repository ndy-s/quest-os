# Visibility & Branding — Japanese Fluency

## Should You Share This?

Sharing a language learning journey publicly is optional but genuinely useful for accountability. The Japanese learning community online is large, supportive, and active — particularly on Twitter/X and YouTube. You don't need to become a content creator; even occasional posts showing progress create enough social accountability to push through dry stretches.

Recommended approach: post when you hit milestones, not on a rigid schedule.

---

## Platform Strategy

**Twitter/X** is the best platform for this. The #learnjapanese and #JLPT communities are active, and short posts about what you're studying get real engagement. You don't need many followers — even 20 people watching creates accountability.

**YouTube/TikTok** is higher effort but higher reach. A short monthly "month X of learning Japanese" video can build an audience quickly if you're consistent. Only worth it if you enjoy making videos.

**No public presence** is also fine. Use a private Obsidian log or a small Discord community instead.

---

## Content Ideas

- "Day 1: starting Japanese from scratch" — sets a public anchor
- "Week 2: I can finally read hiragana" — milestone post, always gets engagement
- "Anki review streak: 30 days" — consistency proof
- "Watched 5 minutes of anime and understood maybe 20%" — honest, relatable
- "JLPT N5 practice test result: X/60" — accountability
- "First iTalki conversation in Japanese" — even if terrible, people love these
